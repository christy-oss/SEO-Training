Phase 2

Client Onboarding:
It's All About ACCESS

From signed contract to ready-to-launch. The single most critical factor in this phase: securing complete access to all client systems and platforms.

🔑
NEW PROCESS — Orange Email Access Management
Each client receives a dedicated Orange 142 email address (e.g., clientname@orange142.com) that serves as the master access point for all SEO/GEO tools and platforms. This single username allows clients to grant us access to everything—GA4, GSC, CMS, hosting, GBP, and more—while we work through vendor assignment and technical validation in parallel.
✍️
Deal Won → Project Created
Signed SOW + setup fee collected triggers Asana project creation, ILID assignment, and Slack notification to the team. Internal GDrive repo established.
📧
Orange Email Provisioned
Orange 142 email created for the client (e.g., clientname@orange142.com). This becomes the single access point for all platforms—no more chasing multiple logins.
📨
Onboarding Email Sent
Sent within 5 days via seo-geo@orange142.com. Instructs client to grant access using the Orange email. Access is a gating item — work cannot begin without it.
🔧
Vendor Assignment (In Parallel)
While access is being granted, vendor assignment begins. Standard WordPress accounts → Vendasta. Enterprise/complex CMS → external SEO specialist. Vendor validates CMS and technical feasibility once access is confirmed.
🔑
Access Received & Validated
All credentials must be received 2 weeks prior to project start date. Account Manager confirms access in Asana. Vendor performs initial technical investigation to identify blockers or unknowns.
📅
Kickoff Meeting Scheduled
30-minute introductory call to set expectations for the first month, confirm content priorities, and ensure all inputs are ready for the Launch Plan phase.
✅
Onboarding Complete
Trigger for Phase 3: All access granted, vendor confirmed, kickoff meeting complete, and internal team mobilized for Launch Plan development.
⚙️ Why the Orange Email Process Works: The single Orange 142 email allows us to immediately begin access setup without waiting for vendor assignment decisions. Clients grant permissions once, to one email, while we simultaneously determine the right delivery team and investigate technical requirements. This eliminates the old bottleneck where access requests stalled until vendor selection was finalized.

Phase 2 · Client-Facing

The Kickoff Meeting

A 30-minute session to set expectations, confirm priorities, and ensure alignment before Launch Plan development begins.

🎯
Meeting Objectives
• Set expectations for the first month of work
• Confirm content priorities and business goals
• Review technical requirements and access
• Identify key competitors and events
• Establish communication cadence and reporting preferences
📋
Key Deliverables from Client
• Top 3–5 content priorities for first 90 days
• Competitor list and any known market shifts
• Upcoming events, promotions, or seasonal priorities
• Brand guidelines, tone preferences, and any content restrictions
• Confirmation of all access credentials
💡
Mostly Templatized: This meeting follows a standard agenda to ensure consistency across all engagements. The Account Manager leads, supported by the Services Lead. Focus is on gathering inputs that feed the Launch Plan, not delivering strategy (that comes in Phase 3).

Phase 4

Monthly Delivery Rhythm

Once live, every month follows a consistent execution and reporting loop. Here's what happens each month across all tiers.

📝
Search Content Production
New articles (700 / 1,400 / 2,800 words/mo by tier) plus existing page refreshes (1–5 pages/mo).
🔧
Technical Schema Work
1–4 hrs/month of code cleanup, schema markup, and AI optimization. Page speed, Core Web Vitals, internal linking.
🔑
Keyword Optimization
5–24 keywords tracked across Google, Bing, and up to 4 AI platforms. On-page tuning, title/meta optimization.
📍
Local & Authority
GBP optimization and monthly promotions. AI Trust & Authority program with high-authority publications (Best tier).
🤖
AI Platform Optimization
Entity mapping maintenance, single-platform (Better) or multi-platform (Best: ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AIO, Copilot).
📊
Monthly Reporting
10–20 metrics tracked. Better/Best get real-time dashboard + quarterly executive report. Best tier gets competitive AI share-of-voice.
